Weekly Gems

Hidden treasures, one per week. Every entry below is a film we believe deserves a far wider audience than it received.

24 February 2026

The Fall (2006)

Tarsem Singh's visually breathtaking odyssey follows a bedridden stuntman who spins an epic tale of adventure for a young girl in a 1920s Los Angeles hospital. Shot across over twenty countries with no CGI, the film is a love letter to the power of storytelling and the imagination. Wildly underseen upon release, it remains one of the most gorgeous films ever committed to celluloid.

17 February 2026

A Man Escaped (1956)

Robert Bresson's austere masterpiece chronicles a French Resistance fighter's meticulous escape from a Nazi prison. Every scrape of a spoon against a door, every whispered exchange becomes unbearably tense. Bresson strips cinema to its essence — sound, gesture, patience — and the result is one of the most gripping films ever made, yet it remains unknown to most modern audiences.

🎬
10 February 2026

Certified Copy (2010)

Abbas Kiarostami's enigmatic romance follows a British writer and a French antiques dealer as they wander through a Tuscan village, their conversation gradually blurring the line between fiction and reality. Juliette Binoche delivers one of her finest performances in a film that asks whether a copy — of art, of love, of life — can hold the same value as the original.

🎬
3 February 2026

Cleo from 5 to 7 (1962)

Agnès Varda follows a pop singer in near-real time as she wanders Paris awaiting the results of a medical test. What begins as the portrait of a vain, anxious woman blossoms into something far deeper — a meditation on mortality, perception, and the act of truly seeing the world around you. A cornerstone of the French New Wave that remains startlingly modern.

🎬
27 January 2026

The Conversation (1974)

Sandwiched between the first two Godfathers, Francis Ford Coppola's intimate thriller about a surveillance expert consumed by guilt is often overlooked. Gene Hackman gives a career-best performance as a man whose professional detachment crumbles when he believes he has recorded evidence of a planned murder. A haunting study of paranoia that feels more relevant with each passing decade.

🎬
20 January 2026

The Wailing (2016)

Na Hong-jin's genre-defying Korean thriller begins as a darkly comic small-town mystery and slowly metamorphoses into something genuinely terrifying. A bumbling police officer investigates a mysterious stranger whose arrival coincides with a wave of brutal killings. The film weaves folk horror, religious allegory, and police procedural into an unforgettable experience that leaves you shaken long after the credits roll.
